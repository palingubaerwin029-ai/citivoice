<ol><li><strong>Executive Summary</strong></li></ol><p><strong>The Problem</strong></p><p>Every day, citizens of Kabankalan City encounter non-emergency community concerns such as damaged roads, broken streetlights, clogged drainage systems, improper waste disposal, and other public infrastructure issues that affect their daily lives. While these concerns need timely attention from the local government, citizens often rely on different reporting channels such as social media, phone calls, or walk-in visits to communicate their concerns. These methods can make it difficult to know where to report, whether the concern has reached the appropriate office, or if any action has been taken.</p><p>Since reports are submitted through multiple disconnected channels, concern records become fragmented, making it challenging for the Public Information Office (PIO) and other concerned offices to consolidate information, monitor the progress of reported concerns, and provide timely updates to citizens. This lack of a centralized reporting and tracking platform reduces transparency, delays coordination among responsible offices, and limits opportunities for citizens to actively participate in improving their communities.</p><p><strong>Proposed Solution</strong></p><p>To address these challenges, the researchers propose <strong>CitiVoice: Public Service Platform</strong>, a citizen-centered web and mobile application that provides residents of Kabankalan City with a centralized platform to report non-emergency community concerns, upload supporting photos, specify incident locations, and monitor the status of their reports in real time. All submitted concerns are received by the <strong>Public Information Office (PIO)</strong>, which serves as the central receiving and coordinating office, enabling it to efficiently record, categorize, monitor, and manage concerns before coordinating them with the appropriate local government offices for action. The platform integrates <strong>intelligent concern categorization & analysis</strong> powered by Meta’s Llama 3.3 model via Groq using natural language processing. CitiVoice also features <strong>real-time WebSocket notifications</strong> to keep citizens and administrators instantly informed, an <strong>automated SLA escalation workflow</strong> to ensure timely resolution of reported concerns, and <strong>interactive mapping</strong> with heatmaps and marker clustering for geographic analysis. The system supports <strong>multilingual access</strong> in English, Filipino, and Hiligaynon, and includes <strong>push notifications</strong> to deliver timely updates to citizens. By centralizing concern management into a single platform, CitiVoice enhances accessibility, transparency, accountability, and operational efficiency while empowering citizens to actively participate in improving public services within their community.</p><p><strong>Target Users</strong></p><ul><li><strong>Citizens of Kabankalan City</strong> – The primary users of the platform who can submit non-emergency community concerns, upload supporting photos, and monitor the status of their reports through a centralized and accessible platform. </li><li><strong>Public Information Office (PIO)</strong> – The administrative user responsible for receiving, reviewing, recording, categorizing, monitoring, and updating the status of reported concerns, ensuring that citizens are informed of the progress of their reports.</li></ul><p><strong>Technology Used</strong></p><p>CitiVoice is developed as a <strong>web and mobile-based platform</strong> using <strong>React.js</strong> for the web application and <strong>React Native (Expo)</strong> for the mobile application, providing accessibility across multiple devices. The system is powered by <strong>Node.js</strong> and <strong>Express.js</strong> for backend processing and <strong>MySQL</strong> for centralized data storage. To ensure data security and controlled access, CitiVoice implements <strong>JSON Web Token (JWT)</strong> authentication and <strong>Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)</strong>, allowing only authorized users to access and manage system information. The platform also integrates <strong>Groq SDK with Meta’s Llama 3.3</strong> for smart concern categorization and analysis, <strong>Socket.io</strong> for real-time WebSocket notifications, <strong>Leaflet</strong> and <strong>React Native Maps</strong> for interactive mapping with heatmaps and marker clustering, <strong>Recharts</strong> for data visualization and analytics, <strong>Tesseract.js</strong> for OCR-based identity verification, and <strong>Helmet</strong>, <strong>express-rate-limit</strong>, and <strong>Bcrypt.js</strong> for enhanced security hardening.</p><p><strong>Expected Impact</strong></p><p>CitiVoice is expected to empower the citizens of Kabankalan City by providing a convenient, accessible, and transparent platform for reporting non-emergency community concerns and monitoring the progress of their reports. By giving citizens, a centralized channel to communicate with the local government, the platform encourages greater civic participation and strengthens public trust through improved transparency and accountability. For the Public Information Office (PIO), CitiVoice is expected to streamline the recording, monitoring, and management of citizen concerns, resulting in more organized records, improved operational efficiency, and better support for data-driven decision-making. Ultimately, the platform contributes to a more responsive, citizen-centered, and digitally enabled public service.</p><ol><li><strong>Innovation</strong></li></ol><ul><li>Why is your project innovative? </li></ul><p>CitiVoice is innovative because it transforms fragmented and traditional concern reporting into a centralized, citizen-centered digital platform that empowers residents of Kabankalan City to actively participate in improving their community. Beyond digitizing concern reporting, CitiVoice integrates <strong>artificial intelligence</strong> – including automated concern categorization using Meta’s Llama 3.3 model, natural language processing, and sentiment-based urgency detection – to intelligently prioritize concerns. The platform also features an <strong>automated SLA escalation system</strong> that monitors resolution deadlines and escalates overdue concerns without manual intervention, along with <strong>real-time WebSocket notifications</strong> that keep both citizens and administrators informed at every step.
